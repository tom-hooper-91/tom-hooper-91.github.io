What is Emotional Intelligence?

Emotional intelligence is the ability to understand and regulate your own emotions as well as understanding the emotions of others. I think it is part empathy, part self-awareness and it’s hugely beneficial for creating and maintaining healthy relationships.

How is it different to IQ?

IQ can be quantifiably measured; EQ is less tangible in the sense that it can’t be measured to the same degree. EQ is more a reflection on your ability to perceive people and how other people perceive you as a person. While it can’t be assigned a number, you could say that EQ can be measured by the success of a person’s relationships and even their happiness and self-awareness. I hesitate to use the term good person but that is what springs to mind.

Why is it important?

It is important for managing your own emotions, thought processes and stress. This in turn will help improve the quality of your communications with others, along with your skills as a leader and team member. Being able to regulate and control your own emotions and emotional responses to different situations can also make you a happier and more fulfilled person.
